Facts about wolves


1-black wolves
Black wolves are not born as a result of natural reproduction between each other
, as a 2008 study at Stanford University found that the mutation responsible for black fur only occurs in dogs,
so black wolves are a result of gray wolves breeding with domestic dogs. This mutation is a dominant trait
like dark hair in humans, and is transmitted to most offspring
It is not entirely clear what is the benefit of black fur for these animals,
as it does not seem to help them hunt or hide, but they show a marked improvement in the tolerance of some injuries
and black wolves are more common in North America compared to the rest of the world.


2-Hybrid wolf
A large percentage of wolves are actually hybrid wolves, so in areas where regular wolves have been largely eliminated coyotes have appeared.
Over the past few years, large numbers of coyotes have moved east to suburban areas and even major cities like New York and Chicago.
Genetic tests conducted on 100 new wolves in the US state of Maine revealed that 22 of them possessed the genes of purebred wolves
and there are also types of bitches that are usually larger than coyotes, but they are smaller than regular wolves
characterized by being extremely skilled, resourceful and able to adapt according to conditions
Surrounding them, but in general they show a fear of human civilization as coyotes,
and they seem to preserve the instinct of a pure wolf herd of hunting and a high level of aggression.


3- Eating the meat of any kind
A common occurrence among wolves is to eat each other's meat, as these carnivores will not miss any opportunity to eat a meal in need
and those wolves that live in some of the most unforgiving natural areas on this planet, may sometimes have to eat sick or injured individuals from The herd
and any wolf that has died is generally a free meal. Wolves caught in traps must be brought in very quickly by hunters or they will be torn apart by other wolves.
When two flocks meet they often fight a grinding battle in which the male leader is killed
Sometimes their corpses are even eaten by their offspring, and this fact negates the belief that wolves do not eat carrion.


4-Great weight
Some of the heaviest wolves can weigh up to 90 kg, and the size of wolves increases exponentially as they are further from the equator
and wolves of tropical regions are often not larger than medium-sized dogsbut wolves of the Far North can exceed (Alaska, Canada, Russia) 55 kg.
The largest wolf ever killed in North America was in Alaska in 1939 with a weight of 80 kg. In the former Soviet Socialist Republic of Ukraine
a more massive wolf with a weight of 86 kg was killed, there are unproven reports of samples exceeding 91 kg
often it may be for male leaders in regions with stable food sourcesand of course these weights
are almost more than the weight of the colossal Mastiff dog, which It is 55--90 kilograms.


5-Rabid wolves
Rabid wolves are very dangerous even though they are not animals that usually catch rabies, but it can be transmitted to them by other species such as raccoons and foxes
Unlike some animals that display lethargy and disorientation, wolves almost instantly enter a state of extreme rage when they become ill.
It is clear that a large number of attacks on humans are directly related to rabies. However,
such incidents have decreased dramatically over the years,but still a small number of them occur every year.
Although there are clearly treatments available for people who are bitten by rabid animals
the wolf's tendency to bite near the head and neck often causes the virus to reach the brain before even seeking medical help.


6-American wolves
wolves in the Americas are less likely to attack humans than anywhere else in the world. There are very few records of wolf attacks in the United States and Canada
but in Europe and Asia wolves are much more ferocious, historical accounts indicate that more than 3,000 people were killed in France between 1580 and 1830 AD on wolf tusks
In the Middle Ages, special structures were built along highways for travelers across Europe to protect from wandering herds
The wolves of India and Russia are also particularly known for claiming a lot of human lives. During World War 1
soldiers from the Allied and Central Powers were sometimes forced to join forces that fought off hungry wolves drawn
by the smell of blood on the battlefield.


7-Eat dogs
Wolves find dogs deliciously tasty, although they are closely related to them (almost the same type) and can mate easily, but many wolves view dogs as prey
In a battle between the two, the wolf will outpace even the large dogs in general, as wolves have larger teeth and a more severe and dangerous bite.
In Russia, where stray dogs have become a serious problem since the fall of the Soviet Union, they have become a staple of wolf food
Often times a wolf will seduce a dog to follow, leading him into an ambush by the rest of the herd. Only the largest and fiercest cattle keepers
like the Caucasian Terrier and the Tibetan Mastiff have a chance to defend themselves from this deadly animal.
